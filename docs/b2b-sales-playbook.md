## The one thing to understand first

The instinct to market to the laid-off employee — the person giving us 4.9 stars — is emotionally
right but commercially useless. They don't choose the vendor. The only sale that matters is to the
**CHRO, the CFO, and the General Counsel** at the company doing the layoff — buyers who have never
experienced the product and get no report on whether it worked.

Every sales conversation answers one question: **"What does choosing a bad outplacement vendor cost
me — and what does choosing a great one protect?"**

The 4.9-star reviews are not the pitch. They are the proof that the pitch is true.

## What keeps the buyer up at night (in order)

1. Getting sued — wrongful termination, age discrimination, WARN Act violations
2. Glassdoor lighting on fire and destroying hiring for two years
3. The people they kept disengaging, quitting, or checking out
4. The CEO or board asking pointed questions about how this was handled
5. Rehiring the same roles six months later at a premium

Incumbents (LHH, Right Management, Challenger Gray) have owned #1 for 60 years and win by inertia.
**#2–5 is where they are catastrophically weak.**

## The four arguments

### 1. "Your outplacement is destroying your Glassdoor rating" (CHRO)
Ratings drop after a layoff and stay down 12+ months. 86% of candidates check employer reviews;
above-average ratings get 20% more job clicks and 16% more applications. The company pays twice:
severance now, higher cost-per-hire for two years.

### 2. "The layoff isn't over — it's still running inside your building" (CHRO/CFO)
Lay off 100 and 900 watch how they're treated. 65% of survivors admit more costly mistakes; 49%
report morale drops; ~$500/month productivity loss per remaining worker. How you treat the leavers
is the loudest signal to the stayers.

### 3. "You're going to rehire some of these people" (CFO)
~5% of laid-off workers are rehired by the same company within 6–10 months. High performers have a
120% higher rehire rate and return at a ~5% premium. Nearly half of mass-layoff companies rehire
for cut roles within a year. Great outplacement keeps the bridge intact.

### 4. "The legal protection you're buying is weaker than you think" (General Counsel — sharpest blade)
Outplacement is consideration for the severance release. Under ADEA/OWBPA (40+), the release holds
only if consideration is genuine. A service participants publicly rate 1.3–2.8/5 is an argument a
plaintiff's attorney will make — that the consideration was illusory. 4.9 stars with unlimited
coaching until placed is defensible consideration. A portal login is not.

## The nuclear differentiator: Until Placed

Every incumbent sells time-boxed programs (30–90 days of access, paid regardless of outcome).
We coach until placement. "You're not paying for platform access. You're paying for outcomes.
We don't consider the engagement complete until your employee has a new job. No one else offers
that. Ask your current provider why."

## Buyer map

| Buyer | Fears most | The argument that lands |
|-------|-----------|------------------------|
| CHRO | Glassdoor collapse, board scrutiny | #1, then #2 |
| General Counsel | Release enforceability | #4 |
| CFO | Hidden costs, rehiring waste | #3 + #2 |
| CEO / Board | ESG / investor perception | Transition quality as disclosure metric |
| Mid-market HR Director | "Do right by these people" | Testimonials, then "ask your vendor for theirs" |

Warmest entry: the CHRO/HR Director who already lived through a post-layoff Glassdoor crater.

## The three entry doors (from the site — match outreach to the moment)

1. **No program** → set it up before the day you need it; standing and ready this week.
2. **Contract renewing** → ask one question first: what did your people rate it? Public reviews, not the vendor survey.
3. **Inherited the vendor stack** (new people leader) → the easiest line item to upgrade; switching takes days, not quarters.

## Channels

- **Sales Navigator:** CHROs/SVPs HR at 500–10,000-employee companies engaging with layoff content in the past 90 days (live intent).
- **Employment attorneys (employer side):** the legal-consideration reframe makes them referral sources into GC offices.
- **Mid-market PE portfolio companies:** recurring restructuring, CFO-led, less entrenched procurement.
- **SHRM conferences / HR forums:** present original outcome data — the only transparent actor in an industry that hides results.
